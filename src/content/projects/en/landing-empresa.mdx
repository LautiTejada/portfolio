## Problem

The company operated with a static WordPress landing page that no one on the technical team wanted to maintain. Every copy or image change required a developer's intervention, creating bottlenecks with the marketing team.

The site loaded in over 5 seconds on mobile devices, had a Lighthouse performance score below 50, and was not responsive at several breakpoints.

## Solution

A landing page was built from scratch with Next.js and Tailwind CSS, connected to Sanity as a headless CMS. The marketing team edits text, images, and sections from the Sanity studio without touching code.

ISR (Incremental Static Regeneration) was implemented so that changes are reflected in production in under 60 seconds without needing a redeploy.

## Technical Decisions

- **Sanity CMS**: chosen for its typed schema (GROQ + TypeScript), the embedded studio, and the free plan being sufficient for the content volume.
- **ISR over SSR**: content changes only a few times per day, so ISR with 60s revalidation offers the best balance between freshness and performance.
- **Tailwind CSS**: allowed prototyping sections directly in components without an intermediate Figma design step.
- **Optimized images**: `next/image` was used with automatic WebP format and native lazy loading.

## Architecture

The project follows a simple structure:

- `/src/app` — Pages with App Router (layout, home, legal)
- `/src/components` — UI components split by section (Hero, Features, Testimonials, CTA)
- `/src/lib/sanity` — Sanity client, typed GROQ queries, and image helpers
- `/sanity` — Sanity Studio configured as an embedded route

Deployment is automatic on Vercel when pushing to `main`. Sanity has a webhook that triggers on-demand revalidation when new content is published.

## Results

- Lighthouse: 98 Performance, 100 Accessibility, 100 SEO.
- Load time on 3G: < 1.2s (LCP).
- The marketing team publishes changes without technical intervention from day one.
- The site generates 30% more conversions (contact form) compared to the previous version, attributed to improved speed and mobile UX.

## What I Would Do Differently

I would use Sanity with visual mode (Presentation API) from the start to give the marketing team real-time preview. I would also integrate analytics from the first week to have a baseline of metrics before the redesign, instead of comparing after the fact.
